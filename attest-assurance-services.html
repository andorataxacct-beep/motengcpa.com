Which Level of Assurance Do You Need?

Financial statement services come in levels, and the differences matter. Each step up provides more assurance to the people relying on your statements, and each step up costs more. Paying for more assurance than your lender or grantor requires wastes money. Providing less than they require gets your statements rejected.

  • A preparation engagement means we prepare financial statements from your records, with no assurance provided. Often sufficient for internal use and some lenders.
  • A compilation means we prepare your financial statements and issue a formal report, without verifying the underlying information. A common request from lenders for smaller credit lines.
  • A review provides limited assurance. We perform analytical procedures and inquiries and report whether anything came to our attention suggesting the statements need material changes. Frequently required by banks, bonding companies, and investors.
  • An audit provides the highest level of assurance. We examine evidence supporting the amounts and disclosures in your statements and express an opinion on whether they are fairly presented. Required by many grantors, larger lenders, bonding programs, and regulatory bodies.

If someone has asked you for one of these and you are not sure which, send us the request. We will tell you exactly what they are asking for, whether a lower-cost option would satisfy them, and what it will take.

Financial Statement Audits

An audit is an examination of your financial statements performed under professional auditing standards, resulting in an independent opinion that lenders, grantors, bonding companies, boards, and regulators can rely on.

Our audit process is built to be thorough without being disruptive:

  • A planning phase where we learn your organization, your systems, and your risks
  • A clear request list provided up front, so your team is never guessing
  • Fieldwork scheduled around your operations, with remote options available
  • Regular communication throughout, so nothing in the final report is a surprise
  • A closing meeting that covers not just the opinion, but what we observed about your controls and processes
  • Delivery on the timeline your lender, grantor, or board requires

We serve organizations that need a first-time audit as well as those looking for a more responsive alternative to their current audit firm. If you have never been audited before, we walk you through the entire process so you know what to expect at every stage.

Reviews and Compilations

Not every requirement calls for an audit. Reviews and compilations satisfy many lender, bonding, and investor requirements at a significantly lower cost.

Our review and compilation services include:

  • Reviewed financial statements for banks, bonding companies, and investors
  • Compiled financial statements for lenders and internal use
  • Preparation engagements for management and owners
  • Comparative statements covering multiple years
  • Supplementary schedules your lender or surety requests
  • Coordination directly with your banker or bonding agent so the deliverable matches the requirement

If your requirement changes over time, for example when a growing credit line pushes your bank from a compilation to a review, we help you step up smoothly.

Independence: Why It Shapes What We Can Do for You

Professional standards require that the CPA firm auditing or reviewing your financial statements be independent of them. In plain terms: we cannot audit books we keep. If Moteng CPA handles your monthly bookkeeping, a different firm must perform your audit or review, and we prepare you for them.

This is why we serve clients in one of two ways:

  • As your independent CPA firm, performing your audit, review, or compilation while another provider or your internal team maintains your books
  • As your accounting and readiness partner, maintaining your books and preparing you for an audit or review performed by another firm

Both roles are valuable. We help you choose the one that fits how your organization is staffed and what your funders and lenders require.

Audit Readiness for Organizations We Do Not Audit

For organizations where we serve as the bookkeeping and advisory firm, we provide audit readiness and audit support instead of the audit itself: cleaning up accounts before fieldwork, preparing schedules and workpapers, and supporting your team through the auditor's requests. Nonprofit organizations can learn more about our nonprofit audit readiness services, built specifically around the areas nonprofit auditors examine most closely.
