Job costing, bookkeeping, WIP reporting, and tax planning for contractors, home builders, tiny home builders, and subcontractors.

Contact Us

You finished the job. The client paid. And you still are not sure whether you made money on it.

That is not a bookkeeping problem. That is a job costing problem, and it is one of the most common reasons construction businesses struggle even when work is steady.

Moteng CPA, PLLC provides construction accounting, tax, and financial advisory services for contractors and construction companies. We are based in San Antonio, Texas, and work with construction businesses locally and across the country. Our clients need more than basic bookkeeping and annual tax preparation: accurate job costs, reliable financial reports, and a tax strategy that reflects how a construction company actually operates.

Know Which Jobs Are Making Money

A profitable company can still lose money on individual projects when labor, materials, equipment, subcontractor costs, and overhead are not tracked correctly.

We help you build a job costing system that shows:

  • The actual cost of each project
  • Estimated versus actual costs
  • Gross profit by job
  • Labor and material overruns
  • Unbilled work and retainage
  • Overhead that should be included in your pricing
  • Cash needed to complete current projects

Accurate job costing helps you identify problems on a single project before they affect your entire business.

Construction Tax Planning and Compliance

Tax planning should happen before the year ends, not after.

We work with construction business owners throughout the year to identify tax-saving opportunities and avoid unexpected tax liabilities. Construction companies also face tax rules that most industries never deal with, including the choice between percentage-of-completion and completed-contract accounting methods and the small contractor exemption under Section 460. Choosing the right method can change when you pay tax on long-term contracts, and we help you get it right.

Our construction tax services include:

  • Business and individual income tax preparation
  • Quarterly estimated tax planning
  • Accounting method review for long-term contracts
  • S corporation tax planning
  • Reasonable compensation review
  • Equipment and vehicle deduction planning
  • Depreciation and Section 179 planning
  • Retirement plan contribution strategies
  • Contractor payment and Form 1099 compliance
  • Entity structure review
  • Multi-state and local tax considerations
  • IRS notice and tax resolution support

Every recommendation is based on your company's financial position, business structure, and long-term plans.

Financial Guidance for Construction Business Owners

Many construction companies appear profitable but struggle with cash because project costs, billing schedules, loan payments, retainage, and taxes are never reviewed together.

We help you understand:

  • How much cash is available after current obligations
  • Whether your projects are priced correctly
  • How overhead affects your profit
  • When your company can afford to hire or purchase equipment
  • How much you should reserve for taxes
  • Whether growth is improving or weakening your financial position

The goal is simple: reliable numbers, so you can make informed decisions before committing to the next project.
