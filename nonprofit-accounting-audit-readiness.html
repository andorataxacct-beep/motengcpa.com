Nonprofit Accounting & Audit Readiness

The auditors are coming, and your team is scrambling. Reconciliations are behind, restricted funds are tangled together, and nobody is sure the grant reports will match the general ledger.

It does not have to be that way. An audit should confirm what you already know about your finances, not reveal it.

Moteng CPA, PLLC provides accounting, audit readiness, and financial advisory services for nonprofit organizations. We are based in San Antonio, Texas, and work with nonprofits locally and across the country. Our structured, step-by-step approach prepares your organization for a smooth, efficient audit before the auditors ever arrive.

How We Approach Audit Readiness

Most nonprofits treat audit preparation as a once-a-year emergency. We help you turn it into a year-round discipline.

Our approach works through the areas auditors examine most closely:

  • Account reconciliations that are complete and current
  • Proper tracking of restricted, temporarily restricted, and unrestricted funds
  • Revenue recognition for grants, contributions, and program fees
  • Documentation and support for significant transactions
  • Internal controls appropriate for your organization's size
  • Grant compliance and reporting alignment
  • Board oversight and financial governance
  • A clean, organized audit package ready for your auditors

Organizations that stay audit-ready year-round spend less on audit fees, respond to auditor requests faster, and avoid the findings that damage funder confidence.

Audit Readiness and Audit Support

When we serve as your bookkeeping and readiness partner, we do not perform your audit. Independence rules require your auditor to be a firm that does not maintain your books, and that is by design. Our role is on your side of the table: preparing your organization so the audit goes smoothly and the results reflect well on your management.

Our audit readiness services include:

  • Pre-audit assessment of your books, controls, and documentation
  • Cleanup of accounts and reconciliations before fieldwork begins
  • Preparation of schedules, workpapers, and the audit package
  • Support responding to auditor requests during the engagement
  • Assistance addressing prior-year findings and recommendations
  • Single audit readiness for organizations spending federal funds
  • Preparation for funder-required financial reviews

If your organization has never been audited and is approaching the revenue or funding thresholds that trigger one, we help you understand what to expect and get ready before the requirement arrives.

Nonprofit Bookkeeping and Accounting

Nonprofit accounting is not small business accounting with different labels. Fund restrictions, grant conditions, functional expense allocation, and donor reporting create requirements that generic bookkeeping misses.

Our nonprofit accounting services include:

  • Monthly bookkeeping and account reconciliation
  • Nonprofit-specific chart of accounts
  • Fund accounting and restriction tracking
  • Grant tracking and compliance reporting
  • Functional expense allocation across program, management, and fundraising
  • Financial statement preparation
  • Budget development and budget-to-actual reporting
  • Bookkeeping cleanup and correction
  • QuickBooks Online setup and support for nonprofits

Every engagement is tailored to your organization's size and funding mix. The goal is books your board can trust and your auditors can verify.

Form 990 and Tax Compliance

Your Form 990 is a public document. Funders, watchdog organizations, and major donors read it before they read anything else about you.

Our nonprofit tax services include:

  • Form 990, 990-EZ, and 990-N preparation
  • Functional expense reporting on the 990
  • Unrelated business income (UBIT) review
  • State charitable registration and filing support
  • Payroll tax and contractor compliance, including Form 1099
  • Support responding to IRS notices

We prepare your 990 so it is accurate, filed on time, and tells your organization's story well.

Financial Guidance for Boards and Executive Directors

Many nonprofit boards approve financial reports they do not fully understand, and many executive directors carry financial responsibility without financial training. That gap is where problems grow.

We help your leadership understand:

  • What your financial statements are actually saying
  • Whether restricted funds are being used correctly
  • How much operating reserve your organization should hold
  • Whether your programs are financially sustainable
  • What auditors and funders look for in your financials
  • How to strengthen board financial oversight

Clear numbers make for confident boards, and confident boards make for stronger organizations.
