Individual & Business Tax Services

Most people find out what they owe in April, when it is too late to change anything.

By then, every planning opportunity from the prior year is gone. The retirement contribution you could have made, the equipment purchase you could have timed differently, the entity election that would have saved thousands. Tax preparation records history. Tax planning changes it.

Moteng CPA, PLLC provides tax preparation, planning, and resolution services for individuals and business owners. We are based in San Antonio, Texas, and work with clients locally and across the country. As both a CPA and an Enrolled Agent, we are licensed to prepare your returns and to represent you before the IRS if you ever need it.

Tax Preparation Done Right

An accurate return is the minimum, not the goal. We prepare your return with an eye toward what it says about next year: where you left money on the table, what changed in your situation, and what to do differently.

Our tax preparation services include:

  • Individual income tax returns (Form 1040)
  • Sole proprietor and single-member LLC returns (Schedule C)
  • S corporation returns (Form 1120-S)
  • Partnership returns (Form 1065)
  • C corporation returns (Form 1120)
  • Rental property and investment income reporting
  • Multi-state tax returns
  • Prior-year and amended returns
  • Tax return review and second opinions

Every return comes with a conversation, not just a copy. We walk you through what your return says and what it means for the year ahead.

Year-Round Tax Planning

The biggest tax savings happen months before the filing deadline. We work with clients throughout the year so that when December 31 arrives, the decisions that lower your bill have already been made.

Our tax planning services include:

  • Quarterly estimated tax planning so there are no April surprises
  • Income timing and deduction strategies
  • S corporation election analysis and reasonable compensation review
  • Entity structure review as your business grows
  • Retirement plan contribution strategies for owners and individuals
  • Equipment and vehicle purchase timing, including Section 179 and depreciation planning
  • Tax implications of major life events: marriage, home purchase, business sale, inheritance
  • Multi-state and local tax considerations

Planning is not a one-time meeting. As tax law changes and your situation changes, your strategy should change with it.

For Business Owners

Your business return and your personal return are not separate problems. How you pay yourself, how your entity is structured, and how you time income and expenses flow through to your personal tax bill.

We help business owners answer questions like:

  • Should my business be an S corporation, and if so, what should my salary be?
  • How much should I set aside for taxes each quarter?
  • Is my current entity structure still right for where the business is headed?
  • What can I deduct, and what documentation do I need to defend it?
  • How do I stay compliant with contractor payments and Form 1099 requirements?
  • What are the tax consequences of bringing on a partner or selling the business?

The answer is never one-size-fits-all. Every recommendation is based on your numbers, your goals, and your tolerance for complexity.

IRS Notices and Tax Resolution

An IRS letter in the mailbox ruins a week. Most notices are fixable, and many are simply wrong, but ignoring them makes every option worse.

As an Enrolled Agent, we are federally licensed to represent taxpayers before the IRS. Our resolution services include:

  • IRS and state notice review and response
  • Penalty abatement requests
  • Payment plan and installment agreement setup
  • Back tax return preparation for non-filers
  • Audit representation and support
  • Account transcript review to find out exactly where you stand

If you have unopened IRS letters in a drawer, you are not alone, and it is not too late. The first step is finding out what the IRS thinks you owe and why.
